Typography Journal
The Problem
Collecting for desktop, tablet and mobile

The first project of our semester was to collect samples of typography on the web, collating screenshots of different type pairings across desktop, tablet and mobile devices. We were asked to arrange our findings into an InVision moodboard organised by device type.

The typographic examples that we found needed to be embedded into the design layout and not from a standalone website such as Google Fonts. Our selections needed to be diverse and not always to our taste; it was more important to just have a good variety of different styles.

The Process
Identifying typefaces and weights

I used my laptop, iPad and smartphone to browse content, and I noticed that layouts designed specifically for tablet were actually quite rare. My examples included:

Serif Types
(Old Style, Modern, Transitional, Slab Serif, Monospace)

Sans-Serif Type Styles
(Geometric, Humanist, Grotesque, Square, Monospace)

Script or Decorative
(Novelty, Hand-drawn)

Weights
(Condensed, Light, Bold, Extra, Roman, Italic, Extended)

I referred to a number of sources for inspiration, ranging from agency websites, restaurant homepages and creative portfolios, to typography newsletters and design blogs.

Three curated InVision boards
