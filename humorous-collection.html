A Humorous Collection
The Problem
Creating within CMS constraints

For our penultimate creative brief, we were asked to curate an interactive collection (images, videos and gifs) that convey a humorous or a satirical concept. The task was to design within the constraints of a content management system (Cargo).

The content had to be an equal balance of self-created work and gathered content found online. Sources for found content could include range from Snapchat, Instagram and Vine to Youtube, Giphy and Imgur, among others.

The Process
Experimenting with non-linear navigation

The topic that I decided to focus on was ‘doors’. I wanted to play with the preconception that a website’s navigation had to be linear and straightforward, and design a web experience that had no real beginning or end.

Focusing on doors was a simple way to encourage users to continue clicking through the site, as a closed or partially open door is naturally a trigger for curiosity and exploration. My content was mostly composed of weird imagery, gifs and memes.

The Solution
Finding humour in ridiculousness

My final website was an endless series of linked pages, all leading to a different door background. Once the user had reached the last of my 20 pieces of content, they were directed back to the beginning and the process repeated itself.

Although not hysterically funny, the absurdity of some of the content I created (such as Drake dancing in a barn) gave the website a humorous undertone. I took advantage of the variety of page animations that the Cargo CMS provided.
